Meditation has been used for all of humanity that helps your mental well-being. It can help with concentration, decreased anxiety, increased awareness, better sleep, improved emotions, decreases rate of disease, and helps you live longer!

Sit comfortablely

An upright posture helps you focus on breathing as you inhale and exhale.

Focus on breathing

Breathe through your nose, keeping your mouth closed, and avoid clenching your jaw or grinding your teeth. Try focusing on how your lungs expand and contract or how it feels when air passes through your nose.

Observe

Don't describe, judge, or analyze, your breathing. Just be "present", focusing on the breathes. If your mind wanders, its fine, bring your attention back to the breath.

Journaling

By writing about something specifically on things that brother you, it can help you release emotions and review your life's situation. The unique way of expressing yourself is a way of self-reflection. This can manage your anxiety, reduce stress, cope with depression, decrease mental distress, and most importantly, help confront painful and stressful events. It can also help one review on what they need to give up or keep in order to have a healthy mental health.

List Journaling

Also known as bulletin-journaling, they are great to be used to organize, track, and record anything ranging from your school-work, to just your thoughts in general. More importantly, it can help you keep on track during the day if used like a todo list.

Art Journaling

Incorporate drawings, paintings, collages, or any other artistic medium into your journal. Use visuals to express your emotions and experiences alongside written words.

Gratitude Journaling

Focus on positive experiences and things you are grateful for. Write down a few things each day or week that you appreciate, helping to cultivate a positive mindset

Reflective Journaling

Use journaling as a tool for self-reflection and personal growth. Write about your thoughts and feelings on specific events or experiences, analyzing their impact on your life.

Dream Journaling

Your dream is your subconcious, and thus is an important factor in understanding your emotions. Write down whatever happens in a dream immediately after for maximum retention. Dreams provide you with underlying truths, new ideas, or aspects from the past. It helps reveal what potiental events that you still not recovered from.

Breathing Techniques

Deep Breathing

Inhale slowly through your nose, counting to four. Feel your chest and abdomen expand as you fill your lungs with air. Hold your breath for a count of four, allowing your body to absorb the oxygen. Exhale gently through your mouth for a count of four, releasing any tension or stress. Repeat this cycle several times, gradually adjusting the pace to what feels most comfortable for you.

Box Breathing

1. Inhale for a count of four.
2. Hold your breath for four counts.
3. Exhale for a count of four.
4. Pause for four counts before beginning the cycle again.

4-7-8 Breathing

1. Inhale quietly through your nose to a mental count of four.
2. Hold your breath for a count of seven.
3. Exhale completely through your mouth to a count of eight.
4. Repeat the cycle.

Body Scan Breathing

1. Inhale and direct your focus to one part of your body.
2. Hold your breath briefly while you observe any sensations.
3. Exhale, releasing tension from that specific area.
4. Move your attention to another part of your body and repeat.
